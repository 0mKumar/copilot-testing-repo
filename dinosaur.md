# What is a Dinosaur?

Dinosaurs are a diverse group of reptiles that first appeared during the Triassic period, approximately 230 million years ago. They dominated the Earth for over 160 million years during the Mesozoic Era before most species went extinct about 66 million years ago.

## Types of Dinosaurs

Dinosaurs are classified into two main groups based on their hip structure:

### Saurischia (Lizard-hipped)
- **Theropods**: Carnivorous dinosaurs that walked on two legs
  - Examples: Tyrannosaurus Rex, Velociraptor, Allosaurus
- **Sauropodomorphs**: Large, long-necked herbivores
  - Examples: Brachiosaurus, Diplodocus, Apatosaurus

### Ornithischia (Bird-hipped)
- **Ornithopods**: Herbivores with specialized teeth for grinding plants
  - Examples: Iguanodon, Parasaurolophus, Hadrosaurus
- **Thyreophora**: Armored dinosaurs
  - Examples: Stegosaurus, Ankylosaurus, Triceratops
- **Marginocephalia**: Dinosaurs with distinctive head crests or frills
  - Examples: Pachycephalosaurus, Triceratops

## Notable Characteristics

- **Size**: Ranged from small bird-sized species to massive creatures over 100 feet long
- **Diet**: Herbivores, carnivores, and omnivores
- **Habitat**: Lived on all continents
- **Legacy**: Birds are considered modern descendants of theropod dinosaurs

## Extinction

The most widely accepted theory for dinosaur extinction is the impact of a massive asteroid approximately 66 million years ago, which caused drastic climate changes and led to the extinction of most dinosaur species, except for the lineage that evolved into modern birds.
